Rauvolfia serpentina

Rauvolfia serpentina, commonly known as Indian snakeroot or serpentwood, is a medicinal plant native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia. It has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and other traditional medicinal systems for its potential health benefits.

One of the main bioactive compounds found in Rauvolfia serpentina is reserpine, which has been of particular interest due to its antihypertensive (blood pressure-lowering) and antipsychotic properties. Reserpine was historically used to treat high blood pressure and certain psychological disorders, but it has largely been replaced by newer medications with fewer side effects.
The plant's traditional uses and potential medicinal properties have led to scientific research exploring its various compounds and their effects on the human body. However, due to its complex chemistry and potential side effects, it's important to consult with a qualified healthcare professional before using Rauvolfia serpentina or any products derived from it for medicinal purposes.
